Clue 4: "I cracked the unbreakable, my mind sharper than a cipher. Though my countrymen were at war, I deciphered their most secret code. My invention went beyond my time, and yet I am not remembered as the hero I should be. Who am I?"

This historical figure is famous for solving one of the greatest cipher mysteries of the 20th century. Though instrumental during World War II, he was not celebrated in his time. His contributions laid the foundation for modern computing. Who is this forgotten genius?

Correct! The answer is "Alan Turing," the British mathematician who cracked the Enigma code during World War II and is considered the father of modern computing. His story remained largely unrecognized during his life. Now, you may proceed to the next clue.
